<a id="1-week-1-the-case-system-as-a-binary-code"></a>

## 1. Week 1: The Case System as a Binary Code

**Goal:** Understand how German "marks" nouns based on their role in a sentence.

**The Prompt:**

> Act as a Philologist. Explain the German **Nominative and Accusative cases** specifically for a logic-driven learner.
>
> 1. **The "Why":** Explain cases as "functional markers" that allow for flexible word order.
> 2. **The Mechanics:** Provide a table showing the transformation of Definite, Indefinite, and Negative articles (der/die/das/die).
> 3. **The Pattern:** Explain why only the masculine gender changes in the Accusative from a linguistic efficiency perspective.
> 4. **Practice:** Provide 10 sentences in English and ask me to identify which noun is the "Subject" (Nom) and which is the "Direct Object" (Acc) before showing the German translation logic.

<a id="2-week-2-the-v2-anchor--separable-bracket-logic"></a>

## 2. Week 2: The V2 Anchor & Separable Bracket Logic

**Goal:** Master the fixed "math" of German word order in main clauses.

**The Prompt:**

> Explain the **V2 (Verb-Second) Rule** in German main clauses as a "mathematical constant."
>
> 1. **The Anchor:** Explain why the conjugated verb is the fixed "anchor" of the sentence and how "Inversion" works if a time or place element starts the sentence.
> 2. **The Bracket:** Explain **Separable Verbs** (trennbare Verben) as a "Satzklammer" (sentence bracket) where the prefix acts as a logical "closer" at the end of the clause.
> 3. **The Logic:** Explain the "Field Model" (Feldermodell) of a German sentence: Vorfeld, Linke Satzklammer, Mittelfeld, Rechte Satzklammer, Nachfeld.
> 4. **Exercise:** Give me 5 sentences to "re-order" based on shifting the starting element while keeping the verb in position 2.

<a id="3-week-3-dative-logic--spatial-geometry"></a>

## 3. Week 3: Dative Logic & Spatial Geometry

**Goal:** Understand the "Recipient" role and the physics of prepositions.

**The Prompt:**

> Deep-dive into the **Dative Case**.
>
> 1. **The "Recipient":** Explain the Dative as the case for Indirect Objects.
> 2. **Two-Way Prepositions:** Explain the **Static vs. Dynamic** logic (Wechselpräpositionen). Use the "Where?" (Wo - Dative) vs "Where to?" (Wohin - Accusative) geometric distinction.
> 3. **The Table:** Show the article shifts (e.g., die -> der, das -> dem).
> 4. **The "Why":** Explain why "in der Schule" (Dative) and "in die Schule" (Accusative) mean fundamentally different things to a German brain based on movement logic.

<a id="4-week-4-subordination--the-syntax-toggle"></a>

## 4. Week 4: Subordination & The Syntax Toggle

**Goal:** Learn how German "packs" information into dependent clauses.

**The Prompt:**

> Explain the logic of **Subordinating Conjunctions** (weil, dass, wenn, obwohl).
>
> 1. **The Verb Kick:** Explain why these specific words "toggle" the conjugated verb to the absolute end of the sentence.
> 2. **The Logic:** Contrast "weil" (subordinating) with "denn" (coordinating) to show how syntax changes even when the meaning is the same.
> 3. **The Comma Rule:** Explain why the comma is a mandatory "logical separator" in German syntax.
> 4. **Exercise:** Provide 5 pairs of sentences to be joined using "weil" or "dass," requiring the "Verb Kick."

<a id="5-week-5-tense-mechanics-haben-vs-sein"></a>

## 5. Week 5: Tense Mechanics (Haben vs. Sein)

**Goal:** The logic of past actions and "Change of State."

**The Prompt:**

> Explain the **Perfekt (Past Tense)** construction.
>
> 1. **The Logic of Auxiliary Verbs:** Why do some verbs take "haben" and others take "sein"? Explain the "Movement/Change of State" rule for "sein" versus the "Transitive" rule for "haben."
> 2. **Participle II:** Explain the "ge-" prefix logic and the difference between "strong" (-en) and "weak" (-t) verb endings.
> 3. **Usage Logic:** Explain when to use Perfekt (spoken/informal) vs. Präteritum (written/narrative) from a linguistic perspective.

<a id="6-week-6-adjective-endings-the-information-transfer-theory"></a>

## 6. Week 6: Adjective Endings (The Information Transfer Theory)

**Goal:** De-mystify the most "hated" part of German grammar using signal theory.

**The Prompt:**

> Explain **Adjective Endings** using "Information Transfer Theory."
>
> 1. **The Theory:** Explain the logic of "Strong, Weak, and Mixed" declension not as memorization tables, but as a system of "Signals." If the article already shows the case (Strong Signal), the adjective doesn't need to. If the article is missing, the adjective must carry the signal.
> 2. **The Flowchart:** Create a logical "Yes/No" decision tree for deciding an adjective ending.
> 3. **Practice:** Provide 10 phrases with missing endings for me to solve using the logic of "Who is carrying the case marker?"

---

<a id="7-week-7-the-modal-bracket--intentionality"></a>

## 7. Week 7: The Modal Bracket & Intentionality

**Goal:** Expressing ability, permission, and necessity.

**The Prompt:**

> Explain **Modal Verbs** (können, dürfen, müssen, sollen, wollen, mögen).
>
> 1. **The Mechanism:** Show how they create a "Frame" where the second verb is always in the infinitive at the end.
> 2. **The Logic of Nuance:** Explain the semantic difference between "müssen" (objective necessity) vs. "sollen" (subjective/social expectation).
> 3. **Subjective Use:** Briefly explain how modals are used to express "probability" (e.g., "Das müsste stimmen").

---

<a id="8-week-8-konjunktiv-ii-the-geometry-of-what-if"></a>

## 8. Week 8: Konjunktiv II (The Geometry of "What If")

**Goal:** Mastering hypotheticals, wishes, and politeness.

**The Prompt:**

> Explain **Konjunktiv II (The Subjunctive)**.
>
> 1. **The "Why":** Why does German shift the vowel (Umlaut) and add endings to indicate "irreality"?
> 2. **The "würde" hack:** Explain the modern "würde + infinitive" construction versus the "original" high-frequency forms (wäre, hätte, käme).
> 3. **Politeness Logic:** Explain why "I would like" (Ich hätte gerne) is structurally more logical in German than English.

---

<a id="9-week-9-passive-voice-the-subject-object-swap"></a>

## 9. Week 9: Passive Voice (The Subject-Object Swap)

**Goal:** Shifting focus from the "Doer" to the "Action."

**The Prompt:**

> Explain the **Passive Voice (Vorgangspassiv)**.
>
> 1. **The Formula:** "werden" (conjugated) + "Partizip II" (at the end).
> 2. **The Logic:** Show how the Accusative Object from an active sentence "promotes" to the Nominative Subject in the passive.
> 3. **State vs. Process:** Explain the logical difference between "The door is closed" (Zustandspassiv) and "The door is being closed" (Vorgangspassiv).

---

<a id="10-week-10-relative-clauses-as-precision-tools"></a>

## 10. Week 10: Relative Clauses as Precision Tools

**Goal:** Embedding detailed information within sentences.

**The Prompt:**

> Explain **Relative Clauses (Relativsätze)**.
>
> 1. **The Pronoun Logic:** How to choose a relative pronoun based on the Gender of the noun it replaces but the Case required by its function in the new clause.
> 2. **Syntax:** Explain why the verb moves to the end.
> 3. **Nesting:** Show how to "embed" a relative clause in the middle of a main clause without breaking the V2 logic of the main sentence.

---

<a id="11-week-11-nominalization-the-b2-academic-engine"></a>

## 11. Week 11: Nominalization (The B2 "Academic" Engine)

**Goal:** Transitioning from "Basic" to "Professional" (C-Level) German.

**The Prompt:**

> Introduce **Nominalization (Nominalisierung)** for a B2 learner.
>
> 1. **Transformation:** Show how to turn a verb (handeln) into a noun (das Handeln) or an adjective into a noun (schön -> die Schönheit).
> 2. **Professionalism:** Explain why B2/C1 German prefers "Preposition + Noun" (Trotz des Regens...) over "Conjunction + Verb" (Obwohl es regnete...).
> 3. **Functional Verb Phrases:** Explain "Funktionsverbgefüge" (e.g., "in Erfahrung bringen" instead of "erfahren") and the logic behind using them.

<a id="12-week-12-complex-connectors--participle-adjectives"></a>

## 12. Week 12: Complex Connectors & Participle Adjectives

**Goal:** Final synthesis and syntactic mastery.

**The Prompt:**

> Final B2 Mastery: **Advanced Connectors and Participles**.
>
> 1. **Position Logic:** Categorize connectors into Position 0 (und, aber), Position 1 (deshalb, trotzdem), and "Verb-End" (obwohl, da).
> 2. **Participle I as Adjective:** Explain the "Present Participle" (e.g., "the sleeping dog" - der schlafende Hund).
> 3. **Synthesis:** Provide a complex B2-level paragraph and ask me to deconstruct every grammar rule used in it to prove I understand the "engine."
