# Ch 9: Conclusion

- **Design is conceptual and practical** — design occurs when great ideas are visualized using type, images, and other materials. Both halves are required.
- **The book is the beginning** — the content covered is just the start of a designer's education; advanced classes will add new skills and new ways of delivering content to users.
- **Learning is a lifelong process** — try to be a sponge and absorb the visual world; soon the world is seen through the eyes of a designer.
- **Designer's gaze** — badly kerned letters become annoying; environmental colors invite use in projects; textures and patterns read as raw material rather than backdrop.
- **Joining a community** — continued practice connects the designer to a community of people committed to solving practical problems and exploring new visual territory.
- **Design is more than a job** — it is a way of life. *Welcome to the world of design.*
