# Get ’er Done Garlic Noodles

> Serves: 4 · Total time: 15 minutes · Total cost: Under $5

## Ingredients

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 8 ounces | thin rice noodles or similar noodles (gluten-free if preferred) |
| ¼ cup | soy sauce (gluten-free if preferred) |
| ¼ cup | brown sugar |
| 2 tablespoons | rice vinegar |
| 4 teaspoons | sesame oil |
| 3 tablespoons | vegan butter |
| 6 | green onions, sliced, divided |
| 8 cloves | garlic, minced |
| 1 tablespoon | sesame seeds, for garnish |

## Preparation

1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and cook the noodles according to package directions.
2. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, sugar, rice vinegar, and sesame oil, and set aside.
3. Melt the vegan butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat then add about two-thirds of the green onions and all of the garlic. Sauté for 1–3 minutes, until fragrant. Add the cooked noodles and the sauce. Toss well and heat through. Garnish with the remaining green onions and sesame seeds and serve hot.
**QUICK TIP:** If you have veggies or tofu on hand, feel free to chop them up and add them to this noodle dish. Bell peppers, carrots, broccoli, peas, and mushrooms all work well. Sauté the additional veg in the vegan butter until tender before adding the onions and garlic.
**MAKE AHEAD:** Store the cooled noodles in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.
