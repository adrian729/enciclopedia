# Leftover Rice Pudding

> Total: 12 mins · Yield: 4 servings · Cost: under $5

## Ingredients

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 2 cups | cooked rice |
| 1 can (14 fluid ounces) | full-fat coconut milk (about 1¾ cups) |
| ¼ cup | white sugar |
| ¼ teaspoon | cinnamon |
| ¼ teaspoon | salt |
| ½ cup | raisins (optional) |
| 1 teaspoon | vanilla extract |

## Preparation

1. Mix together the rice, coconut milk, sugar, cinnamon, and salt in a medium pot. Set over medium-high heat and bring to a simmer. Continue to cook at a simmer for about 5–10 minutes, stirring, until the rice pudding has thickened. Stir in the raisins, if using, and vanilla and heat through for another 2 minutes. If the pudding gets too thick, add a splash of plant-based milk or water to thin. Serve hot or cold.

## Notes

- Quick tip: Full-fat coconut milk makes for an extra-rich pudding, but if you're not a fan of coconut, or you don't have it on hand, use whatever kind of plant-based milk you have. Opt for a plant-based milk higher in fat to keep this pudding creamy.
- Make ahead: You can make the rice pudding ahead of time and store in an airtight container in the fridge for 2–3 days.
