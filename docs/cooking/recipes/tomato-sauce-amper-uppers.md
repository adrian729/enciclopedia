# Tomato Sauce Amper-Uppers

> Total: 15 mins · Yield: 4 servings · Cost: $5–$10 (depending on your choices)

## Ingredients

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 1 jar (20 ounces) | store-bought tomato sauce |
| as desired | cooked pasta of choice (gluten-free if preferred) |

### Before the Sauce

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 1–4 tablespoons | olive oil or vegan butter |
| 1 | onion, chopped |
| 2–6 cloves | garlic, minced |
| 8 ounces | sliced mushrooms |
| 1–3 | carrots, peeled and chopped |
| 1–3 stalks | celery, chopped |
| 1 | bell pepper, chopped |
| 1–2 | zucchini, chopped |

### With the Sauce

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 1–3 teaspoons | Italian Seasoning ([recipe](cooking/recipes/italian-seasoning.md) or store-bought) |
| ¼–½ teaspoon | crushed red pepper flakes or hot sauce |
| to taste | fresh basil, roughly chopped |
| to taste | fresh rosemary, chopped |
| to taste | fresh thyme leaves, chopped |
| 1–3 tablespoons | chopped fresh parsley |
| ¼ teaspoon | liquid smoke or ½ teaspoon smoked paprika |
| 1–3 tablespoons | tomato paste |
| 1–3 teaspoons | white or brown sugar |
| 2–4 teaspoons | capers |
| ¼–½ cup | olives |
| ¼–½ cup | roughly chopped artichoke hearts |
| ½–1 cup | halved cherry or grape tomatoes |
| ¼–½ cup | pasta water |
| ¼–½ cup | chopped walnuts, toasted |
| ¼–½ cup | chopped sun-dried tomatoes |
| 2 large handfuls | greens, such as spinach, arugula, or chopped kale |

### After the Sauce (Garnish to Taste)

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| to taste | fresh basil leaves |
| to taste | fresh parsley, chopped |
| to taste | salt and black pepper |
| to taste | crushed red pepper flakes |
| to taste | nutritional yeast |
| to taste | ground cashews, almonds, or macadamia nuts |
| to taste | roasted and salted sunflower seeds |
| to taste | roasted pine nuts |

## Preparation

1. If cooking any of the "Before the Sauce" ingredients, heat the oil or vegan butter in a large skillet or pot over medium-high heat. Add any of the "Before the Sauce" ingredients. Choose one or as many as you'd like. Once the veggies are sautéed and beginning to brown, pour in the jar of tomato sauce.
2. You can now add any of the "With the Sauce" ingredients. Choose as many or as few of these additions as you'd like and heat the sauce through.
3. Once you are happy with your sauce, toss it with the cooked pasta of your choice or spoon over the pasta. Garnish with any of the "After the Sauce" ingredients.

## Notes

- Quick tip: If your tomato sauce already contains some of the suggested ingredients, it may seem redundant to add more, but fresh ingredients will enhance the sauce and make it taste and look freshly made.
- Make ahead: Allow the sauce to cool before storing in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.
- Favorite combos:
  - **Sun-dried Tomato:** tomato sauce, vegan butter, sun-dried tomatoes, arugula, and walnuts.
  - **Olives & Capers:** tomato sauce, olives, capers, fresh thyme leaves, ground almonds, and nutritional yeast.
  - **Zucchini & Garlic:** tomato sauce, olive oil, zucchini, garlic, basil, salt, and pepper.
- Things to consider when adding "With the Sauce" ingredients:
  - Extra spices and herbs will enhance the flavors.
  - Liquid smoke or smoked paprika will add some smokiness.
  - Tomato paste will give the sauce more depth.
  - Sugar will help cut the acidity of the tomatoes.
  - Capers, olives, or artichokes will give the sauce a tangy punch.
  - Cherry or grape tomatoes add fresh, sweet pops of tomato flavor.
  - Starchy pasta water will loosen the sauce while also making it adhere to the pasta better.
  - Walnuts or sun-dried tomatoes will add some great texture.
  - Wilting in some greens will add color and flavor.
