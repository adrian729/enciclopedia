# Cajun-Spiced Veggie Pasta

> Total: 25 mins · Yield: 4 servings · Cost: under $10

## Ingredients

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 14 ounces | short pasta, such as cavatappi, penne, or rigatoni (gluten-free if preferred) |
| ¼ cup | roughly chopped almonds |
| 1 tablespoon | olive oil |
| 8 ounces | button mushrooms, sliced |
| 2 medium | zucchini, sliced |
| 1 | red onion, thickly sliced |
| 2 cloves | garlic, minced or pressed |
| 1 cup | halved grape tomatoes |
| 1 tablespoon | Cajun Seasoning ([recipe](cooking/recipes/cajun-seasoning.md) or store-bought) |
| ¼–½ cup | reserved pasta water |

## Preparation

1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and cook the pasta according to package directions. Before draining the pasta, reserve ½ cup pasta water and set aside.
2. Add the almonds to a small dry frying pan and set over medium heat. Toast the almonds until they just begin to brown, shaking the pan as needed. Remove from heat and set aside.
3. In a large skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. When hot, add the mushrooms, zucchini, onions, and garlic. Cook for about 10 minutes, until the veggies begin to brown. Stir in the tomatoes, Cajun seasoning, and ¼ cup reserved pasta water and heat through for 2 minutes more.
4. Add the pasta and toss well, adding the remaining ¼ cup pasta water if needed to help the sauce coat the noodles. Garnish with toasted almonds.

## Notes

- Make ahead: Once cooled, the pasta can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.
