# Biscuit Mix

> Total: 25 mins · Yield: just over 4 cups (4 batches biscuits) · Cost: under $3

## Ingredients

### For the mix

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 4 cups | all-purpose flour |
| 3 tablespoons | baking powder |
| 2 teaspoons | white sugar |
| 1 teaspoon | salt |

### For the biscuits (makes 6)

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 1 cup | Biscuit Mix |
| 3 tablespoons | cold vegan butter |
| ¼ cup | plant-based milk, such as soy or oat |
| 1 teaspoon | lemon juice or apple cider vinegar |

## Preparation

### To make the mix

1. Combine flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt in a large jar or sealable container; whisk or shake to distribute evenly. Store in the pantry for up to 1 year.

### To make the biscuits

1. Preheat the oven to 450°F. Lightly grease a large baking sheet or line with parchment.
2. **Food processor:** Pulse 1 cup biscuit mix and the cold vegan butter until sandy. Add the plant-based milk and lemon juice; pulse just a few times until a shaggy dough forms with some unmixed powder remaining. **By hand:** Cube the cold butter, scatter over 1 cup biscuit mix in a bowl, and cut in with a pastry cutter or fork until sandy. Add the plant-based milk and lemon juice; mix to a shaggy dough leaving some unmixed flour.
3. Turn the dough onto a clean surface and gently knead just until incorporated. Pat into a disk about ¾ inch thick. Cut with a 3-inch round cutter (or knife/glass), gathering scraps as needed to make 6 biscuits.
4. Place biscuits on the prepared sheet and bake 8–10 minutes, until risen and golden on the bottoms.

## Notes

- When cutting, press straight down and pull straight up; twisting seals the edges and prevents proper rise.
- Make ahead: cool baked biscuits completely, then freeze airtight for 2–3 months. Thaw at room temperature; rewarm if desired by wrapping in a damp paper towel and microwaving 20–40 seconds.
