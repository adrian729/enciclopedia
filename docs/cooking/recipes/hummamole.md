# Hummamole

> Total: 15 mins · Yield: about 4 cups · Cost: under $7 (dip only) · Equipment: food processor

## Ingredients

| Quantity | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 1 can (19 ounces) | chickpeas, drained and rinsed (about 2 cups) |
| 2 | avocados, peeled and pitted |
| ¼ cup | lime juice (about 2 limes) |
| 3 tablespoons | olive oil |
| 2 tablespoons | tahini |
| 2 cloves | garlic, peeled |
| ¾ teaspoon | salt |
| ¼ teaspoon | black pepper |
| 1 small handful | fresh cilantro, roughly chopped, for garnish (optional) |
| ½ teaspoon | crushed red pepper flakes, for garnish (optional) |
| for serving | Tortilla chips, crackers, pita triangles, or veggies (gluten-free if preferred) |

## Preparation

1. Add the chickpeas, avocados, lime juice, oil, tahini, garlic, salt, and pepper to a food processor and blend until completely smooth and creamy, stopping to scrape the sides as needed.
2. Scoop the dip into a bowl and garnish with cilantro and red pepper flakes, if desired. Serve with your favorite dipper—tortilla chips, crackers, pita triangles, and veggies all work well.

## Notes

- Make ahead: The lime in this recipe not only adds zing, but also keeps the avocado fresh, so you can store this dip in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days.
