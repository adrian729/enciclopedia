## 1. Why Twitter Works for Generative Art

- **Tolerant platform history** — permissive API policies attracted generated poetry, art, and fiction alongside spam.
- **Short format** — tweets gain strength from the surrounding timeline; a fragment can evoke more than a complete work elsewhere would.
- **Cost of a fragment is low** — less pressure on self-consistency; readers happily extend tweets with imagination.

## 2. Worldbuilding Bots

- **@neighbour_civs** — every three hours: *"The Wasteland-Lizard Tribe. A great poet of theirs composed an epic about the Great Rancher. They colour their snouts with red paste."* Name + two details is enough; no cross-tweet consistency required, but each tweet's evocative hooks (*who is the Great Rancher?*) expand the world in the reader's head.
- **@unfamiliar_city** — travel-guide tweets linking to a site with richer generated pages: useful phrases, exchange rate, 7-day forecast, specific restaurants. Internal consistency is impressive (*Dadoe* is both the restaurant in a tweet and the translation of "tasty" elsewhere). The tweet is a portal into a deeper generator.

## 3. Image, Portal, and Narrative Bots

- **@soft_landscapes** — pastel mountainscapes every six hours. Each image is an isolated world evoking what lies beyond the frame. The probability space includes rare dark or saturated views, and sometimes fog so dense no mountains are visible — which, in context, reads as *"oh, it's very foggy there"*.
- **@tiny_star_fields** — tweets that are arrangements of Unicode glyphs (✺ ✹ · * ⋆ ✦ etc.). 180 characters of glyphs prompt the reader to imagine a night sky, a Milky Way, desert camping under the stars.
- **@skeletonsday** — emoji skeletons living adventurous little lives: *"Skelly says no to a frog. Skelly goes to SeaWorld. Skelly discovers aliens."* Overloaded emoji context carries whole narratives in a few characters.
- **@ArpObservatory** — bio: *"In July we started receiving audio signals from outside the solar system, and we've been studying them since."* Every ~30 minutes: generated waveforms, SoundCloud audio links, waveform commentary, or personal observations (*"I just watched a 'lava lizard' basking by the salt flat"*, *"Gazing up at the clear night sky, each star appears so close"*). Taken as a feed the templates show; taken interleaved with other tweets, the account seeds an ongoing sense of lonely purpose and everyday beauty from the Atacama. The rare reflective tweets feel special precisely because they're rare; ambiguity about whether the signals are alien keeps the mood intact.
- **Metadata matters** — profile, bio, location, website, header image set first impressions. Time spent framing can outperform algorithm tuning.

## 4. Jokes, Memes, and Protest

- **@vg_erotica** — hot breathless prose followed by a videogame cliché. Tweet length is perfect: long enough to set a rhythm, short enough to interrupt it. Random sampling from "erotic" and "videogames" juxtaposes — sometimes flat, occasionally gold, on average good enough.
- **@darndesttruisms** — combines *"my small child said a thing which backs up my political arguments"* with Jenny Holzer truisms. Simpler than @vg_erotica; elicits unease and interrogates rhetoric.
- **@everyword** — posted every English word over seven years. The 7-year setup built a punchline: after *"zymurgy"*, the bot posted *"éclair"* — the word list sorted non-ASCII characters after "z". The slow-build twist followed naturally from the setup but surprised everyone.
- **Darius Kazemi's meme-death bots** — *@choppermemebot* (American Chopper format with movie scripts), *@expandingbot* (expanding-brain macros), *@BracketMemeBot* (elimination brackets from Wikipedia categories). Automating a meme at saturation hastens its death — a courtesy to feeds.
- **@everyboolean** — posted "true", then years later "false". The content was inferrable but the timing wasn't; two tweets establish a pattern.
- **Protest bots** — Everest Pipkin (creator of *@tiny_star_fields*) announced that from now on they'd make only protest bots and built **@Abolish_ICE_Now**, which lists US detention facilities with statistics, satellite photos, and Spanish translations. The bot doesn't pretend to be anything but a bot; template visibility reinforces its cold anger.
- **Ethical responsibility** — by making a bot you create an autonomous speaker in a shared medium. Filter corpora for slurs, don't @-notify people without consent. Think about your relationship to the platform.

## 5. Broken Bots and the Durational Aspect

- **@everyletter123** — stopped after tweeting A, B, C. The letter "D" is impossible: *"D username message"* is Twitter's old SMS shorthand for a direct message. Buckenham pictures the bot politely retrying every day.
- **@wa_k** — got stuck tweeting *"吐 — MEANING: disgorge, vomit, confess PRONUNCIATION: to, ha(ku)"* six times a day until it finally broke.
- **Transparency invites personhood** — a well-functioning bot feels like the creation of a person; a broken, forgotten bit of code valiantly disgorging six times a day inspires wonder.
- **Twitter as duration** — tweets persist for long stretches; art can exist as an ongoing process until it becomes background. *@everyword* lived in timelines; *@everyboolean* lived as perpetual suspense. Responding to the platform's rhythms is part of the craft.
