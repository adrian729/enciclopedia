## 1. Look for decisions you wouldn't have made

- **Ask the key question on any design you like** — "Did the designer do anything here that I never would have thought to do?" The unintuitive choices are where new ideas come from.
- **Examples of non-obvious moves** — inverting the background color on a datepicker, positioning a button *inside* a text input instead of outside it, using two different font colors within a single headline.
- **Why it works** — paying deliberate attention to these small deviations from the default is how you build a library of tricks to apply to your own work.

## 2. Rebuild your favorite interfaces

- **Recreate designs from scratch, without peeking at DevTools** — the act of trying to match the original forces you to notice the details that make it polished.
- **Tricks you discover this way** — "reduce line height for headings," "add letter-spacing to uppercase text," "combine multiple shadows." Things you'd never read in a tutorial but that you uncover by chasing down the gap between your version and the original.
- **Ongoing practice compounds** — continually studying the work that inspires you with a careful eye keeps adding design tricks to your toolbelt for years.
