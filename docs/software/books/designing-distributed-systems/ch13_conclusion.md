## 1. A New Beginning?

- **Every company is becoming digital** — APIs and services must be delivered to mobile apps, IoT devices, and autonomous systems, demanding redundancy, fault tolerance, and high availability alongside rapid iteration.
- **Demand outstrips supply** — the need for distributed systems has grown by an order of magnitude, yet building them remains too costly and the pool of skilled engineers too small.
- **Historical parallel** — past leaps (compilers, high-level languages, object-oriented programming, managed code) widened the developer pool by crystallizing expert knowledge into reusable patterns and abstractions.
- **Containers and orchestration as the next leap** — these tools, paired with the patterns in this book, can democratize distributed-systems development and grow the population of capable builders.
- **Patterns as the foundation** — sidecars, ambassadors, sharded services, FaaS, work queues, and the others form a shared vocabulary; developers should collaborate on reusable canonical implementations rather than rebuilding from scratch.
- **Outcome** — reuse of these patterns is what will let the industry meet today's demand for reliable, scalable APIs and unlock new classes of applications.
