## 1. How and Why Breech Occurs

- **Normal turning window** — between weeks 32 and 37 the baby rotates from upright into vertex position (head down at the mouth of the cervix), drawn by gravity because the head is the heaviest part. The turn often happens during sleep and goes unnoticed
- **Fear and tension can delay the turn** — if the mother is experiencing fear, tension, or emotional upset, the uterus remains taut and the baby lacks adequate space to complete the rotation. Some mothers are reluctant to "let go," which keeps the uterine muscles tight
- **Breech presentation** — the baby's buttocks remain at the neck of the uterus. Partial rotations can leave a shoulder, an arm, or one or both feet at the lower cervix
- **Options when breech persists** — help the baby turn, birth vaginally in breech position (few medical providers are trained for this, but many homebirth midwives do it), or resort to surgical birth. Caesarean should not be the first avenue explored

## 2. Hypnotherapy for Turning

- **Mehl-Madronna study** — 200 women with babies in breech position at 36+ weeks (baseline spontaneous turn rate after week 37: ~12%). 100 received hypnotherapy; 100 did not (some in the comparison group had ECV). Result: 81 of 100 hypnotherapy babies turned to vertex vs. 26 of 100 in the comparison group (plus 20 more turned via ECV)
- **What the hypnosis session involves** — deep relaxation through guided imagery; the mother visualises her baby easily turning and settling into vertex position; suggestions that the uterus becomes pliable and relaxed to give the baby room; the mother talks to her baby, encouraging release from the breech position
- **Fewer sessions than expected** — originally estimated at ~10 hours per mother; actual average was 4 hours, and half of the successful turns required only one session
- **Release therapy** — mothers with breech-presented babies can be helped to identify and release negative emotions that may be contributing to uterine tension. When the turn is achieved through relaxation and tension release rather than manual manipulation, the baby usually stays in vertex position
- **ECV babies may revert** — babies turned through external cephalic version (manual manipulation from outside the abdomen) not uncommonly turn back into breech

## 3. Other Turning Techniques

- **Practitioner resources** — a HypnoBirthing practitioner can help locate acupuncturists, acupressurists, chiropractors who perform the Webster technique, and reflexologists experienced with turning techniques
- **"Tilting" techniques** — positional methods to encourage the baby to turn; inquire about these through your practitioner
- **ECV as last resort** — external cephalic version is not a procedure of choice for most women, but it is preferable to surrendering to a surgical birth without trying other options first
