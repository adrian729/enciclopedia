## 1. Why Labour Slows and Why That Is Normal

- **Slowing and restarting is a normal pattern** — labour commonly accelerates, slows, rests, and then accelerates again. This is not a trouble sign. The worst response is to panic and accept augmentation (Syntocinon drip, membrane rupture) as an automatic next step
- **Patience is the primary tool** — the best way to manage a resting labour is simply to wait. After a calm nap, many mothers resume an active and even accelerated labour
- **"Hugs before drugs"** — ask for privacy and time to use natural methods before agreeing to any intervention. As long as mother and baby are healthy and strong, protect the baby from unnecessary drugs
- **Rupturing membranes or Syntocinon may not help** — occasionally, when a mother accepts these interventions, labour does not move along more rapidly. Unless there is a true medical urgency, the family should pause and weigh the effects on both mother and baby before consenting

## 2. The EFM Problem

- **Electronic Foetal Monitors (EFM) were designed for high-risk labours** — introduced in the 1980s for women with complications, EFMs are now in every room and are a significant drawback for low-risk families
- **Caregivers fixate on tracings** — some providers become overly caught up with EFM patterns and believe the cervix must open on a regulated schedule. When it doesn't, they intervene to "fix" the labour, and the baby often ends up worse off from the fixing itself
- **HypnoBirthing families avoid this trap** — because they typically do not have continual EFM, they are better positioned to ride out a resting labour without being pressured into augmentation

## 3. Natural Methods to Pass Time and Restart Labour

- **Birth ball** — offers an alternative to staying in bed; supports the mother while the birthing companion applies Light Touch Massage; relaxes pelvic muscles. Many hospitals provide them, or bring your own
- **Bath or birth pool** — place a towel from nipple line to thighs to keep protruding body parts warm; the birthing companion scoops water over the body and recites prompts during surges
- **Warm shower** — direct the water stream to the abdomen for an effleurage-like effect
- **Humour** — laughter produces deep breathing that relaxes the body, increases endorphin production, and blocks catecholamine. Pack humorous reading material
- **Nipple stimulation** — stimulating one or both nipples triggers the hormonal connection between breast and vagina, producing natural oxytocin that enhances uterine surges. Ask for privacy to use this method; caregivers will not be surprised by the request

## 4. When Medical Assistance Becomes Necessary

- **Recognising the threshold** — if all natural accommodation has been extended but a clear pattern of decreased or severely weakened uterine activity forms and the baby is not weathering it well, medical assistance may be needed
- **Stay on the decision-making team** — being part of the discussion about next steps helps you remain calm and in control even when the plan changes. Accepting necessary intervention with informed consent is still HypnoBirthing
