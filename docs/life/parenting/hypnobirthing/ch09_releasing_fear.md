## 1. Why Fear Release Matters Before Labour

- **Unresolved fears surface during labour** — brushing aside worries may get you through pregnancy, but suppressed concerns can easily surface as fears during labour and affect the course of your labour
- **Dick-Read's foundation** — educating women in the true physiology of their birthing muscles and the need to be free of tension was the backbone of Dick-Read's work; women who understood this were able to birth unmedicated, alert, and largely free of discomfort
- **Both partners should participate** — it is helpful for both the mother and her partner to identify painful feelings, experiences, or recollections that could limit the ability to approach birth free of harmful emotions

## 2. Common Areas of Concern

Based on Dr. Louis Mehl-Madronna's study on turning breech babies with hypnosis, the following areas commonly surface for pregnant women:

- **Your own birth story** — if what you have been told about your own birth is frightening, recognise that you are not your mother; this is a different pregnancy, a different time, and different preparation
- **Others' birth stories** — family members may impress "family patterns" of long labours, severe pain, or intervention; there is no reason to assume their experiences will be yours. Decline to absorb their past baggage
- **Previous labours** — if a prior birth was difficult, take hope that better preparation and HypnoBirthing skills can produce a different outcome; actively release memories of previous births
- **Parenting confidence** — if you lacked good role models, recognise that poor models can teach you what you would not want to do; turn it into a positive factor
- **Support network** — assess whether your partner and family will share responsibilities; sometimes simply voicing the need for support resolves the matter
- **Marriage/relationship security** — evaluate whether your relationship is strong enough to weather raising a child; working together in HypnoBirthing can strengthen the bond
- **Career ambivalence** — sort through whether you will return to work or stay home; reconciling these feelings before birth removes a source of background stress
- **Housing and space** — determine whether practical accommodations can be made for the baby; expressing the need often opens paths to change
- **Medical care provider** — confirm that your provider genuinely supports your plan for natural birth; ask directly and assess whether your decisions are fear-based or confidence-based
- **Finances** — if money is a worry, address it head-on rather than letting it simmer as background anxiety
- **Prior relationships** — release hurtful thoughts from earlier relationships through a fear-release session so they do not intrude during labour
- **Personal experience of abuse** — memories of physical or sexual abuse are deeply body-associated and can surface powerfully during birthing, causing the body to shut down or resist. It is important to do release work with a qualified hypnotherapist before advancing further into pregnancy

## 3. How to Do the Work

- **Inventory your fears honestly** — search your inner feelings to discover areas of confidence and areas that need resolution; do not skip this step
- **Talk it through** — discuss concerns with your birthing companion, a trusted friend, or your HypnoBirthing practitioner; the practitioner will guide fear-release sessions in class
- **Hypnotherapy for stubborn fears** — if class sessions and conversations are not enough, request a private fear-release hypnotherapy session with your practitioner or a qualified hypnotherapist; this is one of the most effective methods for eliminating toxic emotions
- **Mind and body must be in harmony** — take this assessment seriously; your mind and body work best when both are free of limiting thoughts and emotions so you can approach birthing with trust and confidence
