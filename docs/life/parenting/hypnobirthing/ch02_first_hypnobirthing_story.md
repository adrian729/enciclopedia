## 1. The Setting

- **January 1990, Concord, New Hampshire** — Mongan drove to her daughter Maura's house at 5:30 a.m. for the birth of the first HypnoBirthing baby
- **Three pioneer mothers** — Maura and two friends had all studied HypnoBirthing together and were due around the same time; no precedent existed for any of them to follow
- **Maura's demeanour** — perfectly relaxed, smiling, conversant, tidied her house before leaving for the hospital. No sign of fright or nervousness

## 2. Early Labour and Hospital Admission

- **Admitted below "active labour"** — the nurse assessed Maura as not yet in active labour, but Maura assured her she was experiencing regular surges. She was admitted based on her own report
- **Midwife of choice unavailable** — Maura's regular midwife was not on duty; she was attended by a midwife she didn't know. True to the HypnoBirthing affirmation "I will accept whatever turn my birthing takes," the news did not stress or upset her
- **"Stalled" progress is normal** — Maura stayed at the same level of opening for nearly four hours. Mongan notes this is not uncommon for HypnoBirthing mothers: the body labours but there is little visible change, then they can suddenly register a greater degree of opening in a much shorter time

## 3. Active Labour and What Went Right

- **Trust your body over external direction** — when the nurse urged Maura to squat and walk to "speed up" labour, she tried briefly, then said "No thank you. I need to go back to relaxing now." She was tuned into her body and realised the pace of her labour was stepping up; she needed to relax into her birthing body
- **Handling fear triggers** — after hearing a woman screaming for hours in the adjacent room, Maura opened her eyes and asked: "Am I going to get to that stage?" The nurse replied: "Heavens no. You are way beyond that stage"

## 4. What Went Wrong: Forced Pushing and Episiotomy

- **Directed pushing** — shortly after 1:00 p.m., Maura was completely opened and the midwife advised her to begin to forcefully push
- **Aggressive perineal stretching** — the midwife strenuously stretched the perineum, sometimes with both hands. Maura was obviously in pain and called out to Mongan to have the midwife stop. The midwife continued
- **Episiotomy performed** — the midwife performed an extensive episiotomy, saying "This will help the baby come out faster." Mongan notes this is now seldom performed in most hospitals; birth attendants have learned that the folds of the perineum open by themselves and that the majority of cases call for patience to allow the natural expulsive reflex to move the baby down to crowning
- **Kyle born at 3:30 p.m.** — Kyle Patrick Geddes, the first HypnoBirthing baby, was born. Mongan notes they had no idea thousands, even hundreds of thousands, would follow across over 48 countries

## 5. Takeaways from This Birth

- **Preparation works even in imperfect conditions** — Maura had no precedent, no advantage of watching natural births on the Internet or in films, no happy birth stories to draw from, and not even her chosen midwife. She still achieved a calm labour through preparation and trust in the process
- **Importance of knowing how you want to birth** — Mongan includes this story because it reveals the importance of parents taking responsibility for knowing how they want to experience the arrival of their babies, whether in a hospital, birthing centre, or home
- **Active Management of Labour was already encroaching** — some practices of Active Management of Labour, already with a foothold in other countries, were creeping into American births almost without notice. Hospital births were far from natural; women routinely expected and demanded medication out of apprehension. Mongan asks: is it not possible to accommodate the women who want less or none, as it is those who demand more?
- **"If this could happen for her, it could happen for you"** — Maura knew she wanted things to go differently, and they did. With the education, preparation, and choices available today, Mongan says it can easily happen for you too
