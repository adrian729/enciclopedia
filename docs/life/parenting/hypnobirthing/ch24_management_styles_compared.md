## 1. Four Management Models

- **Spectrum of control** — labour management ranges from fully provider-controlled to fully parent-controlled across four models: Active Management of Labour (AML), Standard/Conventional, Instinctive-Physiological, and Parental Self-Management (Unassisted/Pure Birth)
- **Why this matters** — parents who are well prepared for natural birth can be blindsided by protocols they did not expect. Understanding which model your provider follows lets you identify mismatches before labour, not during it

## 2. Active Management of Labour

- **Origin** — developed in late 1960s–70s Ireland to reduce caesarean sections by preventing long labours. The original "normal" labour was defined as up to 24 hours; over time this was shortened to 12 hours
- **Outcome** — AML has not decreased the caesarean rate; it has had the opposite effect
- **Core philosophy** — a concerted effort to forcefully cause the birthing body to do what it already has the innate ability to do with ease, comfort, and dignity if left undisturbed
- **Practical implication** — if your provider subscribes to AML, expect scheduled induction, early and frequent augmentation, mandatory IV, continuous electronic foetal monitoring, total food restriction, routine epidural, coached forced pushing, routine episiotomy, and immediate cord clamping

## 3. Standard or Conventional Management

- **Blend of approaches** — the most common model in hospital settings; mixes elements of AML with some instinctive-physiological practices. The exact balance depends entirely on the individual birth attendant's philosophy
- **Key difference from AML** — parental input is possible (not guaranteed), some interventions are "may/may not" rather than mandatory, and a midwife may attend alongside or instead of a physician

## 4. Management for Instinctive Birthing

- **Managed only when needed** — providers who subscribe to this model honour parents' requests for unmedicated, unintervened births and intervene only when a medical indication arises
- **Team approach** — parents and care provider work together with mutual understanding: the provider supports the birth plan, and parents agree to cooperate fully if genuine special circumstances require intervention
- **Setting** — typically free-standing birth centres or home; midwife-parent team rather than physician-managed
- **Outcomes described** — births are calm, joyful, and peaceful; babies born this way are described as mellow, content, and easily cared for

## 5. Management Comparison Chart

| Aspect | Active Management | Standardised | Natural/Instinctive | Pure Birth (Unassisted) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Setting** | Hospital | Hospital or birth centre | Free-standing centre or home | Home |
| **Attended by** | Physician managed | Physician/Midwife | Midwife/Parent team | Unassisted |
| **Parent input** | Little/None | Possible | Parents/Provider team | Parents manage |
| **Labour onset** | Induction scheduled | May/May not induce | Spontaneous | Spontaneous |
| **Membranes** | Early rupture | Release of membranes | Spontaneous ROM | Spontaneous ROM |
| **IV access** | Mandatory IV/Heplock | May possibly decline IV | No needle insertions | No needle insertions |
| **Monitoring** | Continuous EFM | Mandatory/Intermittent EFM | Manually monitored | Self-monitored |
| **Labour continuity** | Fragmented | Fragmented | Labour is a continuum | Labour is a continuum |
| **Food** | Total restriction | Possible restriction | Allowed | Allowed |
| **Augmentation** | Early and frequent | Augmentation, possibly frequent | Natural, only as needed | Natural, only as needed |
| **Time pressure** | Chronological insistence | Some chronological expectation | Clock is irrelevant | Clock is irrelevant |
| **Pain management** | Routine epidural | Epidural available | No epidural | Natural relaxation |
| **Pushing** | Coached, forced | Coached, forced | Body's natural expulsive reflex | Body's natural expulsive reflex |
| **Foetal heart decels** | Frequent | Frequent | Less frequent or none | Less frequent or none |
| **Post-birth oxytocics** | Used | Probable | Not used | Not used |
| **Infant suctioning** | Routine | Probable | Only if necessary | Only if necessary |
| **Episiotomy** | Routine | Infrequent | Infrequent | None |
| **Cord clamping** | Immediate | Probable immediate | Delayed | Delayed |
| **Water birth** | Land births only | Probably land births | Water and land births | Mother's choice |
