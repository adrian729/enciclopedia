## 1. The Non-Secret of Natural Birth

- **No secret at all** — the biggest secret about birth is that there is no secret; natural birth advocates have been stating the evidence for decades, and animal mothers demonstrate it every day — they need no explanations of anatomy, no fractionalised stages of labour, and no one cheering them on to push
- **The body's design is complete** — the female body is created to conceive, nurture the development of, and birth a baby; absent unanticipated special circumstances, the precision of the reproductive system during pregnancy and birth has been brilliantly provided by nature
- **"Quadruped" dismissal** — when Mongan asked a medical student how animal mothers achieve quiet, peaceful births in a relaxed state, the student replied with stunning conviction: "The difference is that animal mothers are quadrupeds. Human mothers can't birth that way." Mongan notes that parents today are becoming aware that this kind of rationale will no longer suffice
- **Parents are no longer satisfied with dismissal** — today's birthing families insist that a healthy mother and a healthy baby are not the only things that matter; the manner in which the birth is handled matters too, and they want a birth that is fulfilling and respectful of the dignity of mother, baby, and the experience itself

## 2. What a Birthing Mother Needs

| Need | Practical Detail |
|---|---|
| **Safety and comfort** | Kind, caring, respectful attendants; removal of unnecessary apparatus (raised bed rails, visible machinery) that signals danger |
| **No unnecessary IV/heplock** | The birth companion or doula can manage fluid intake and output |
| **Quiet, undisturbed environment** | Minimal interruptions so the mother can maintain focus and connection with her baby |
| **Individualised care** | Treatment based on the status of her own health, not blanket protocols from other mothers' conditions |
| **No fear-inducing comments** | Unnecessary remarks about what "could" happen later in labour can shut labour down |
| **Freedom of movement and position** | Positions and activities based on the mother's preferred comfort; soft "nesting with pillows" to support relaxation |
| **Minimal interruptions** | Each break in focus changes the rhythm and flow of labour |
| **Breathing baby down, not coached pushing** | The natural expulsive reflex and her own breathing move the baby; quiet prompts come only from her birth companion |
| **No arbitrary time constraints** | When the baby is tolerating labour well and shows no distress, advanced opening can occur gradually even when it appears sluggish |
| **Private post-birth bonding** | At least one hour of quiet, private family time in the "afterglow" so the baby can adjust to life outside the womb with only immediate family touch |

## 3. The Systemic Failure to Listen

- **Eyes open, minds closed** — many birth professionals witness calm, gentle births every day but choose to ignore, marginalise, and ultimately dismiss them, opting instead for procedures and protocols that are less beneficial, less gentle, less effective, and less psychologically and emotionally sound
- **Stranger as care provider** — the 2010 "Listening to Mothers" survey indicated that half of responding birthing mothers met their care provider for the first time as he or she walked into the birthing room
- **"People who want those kinds of births should stay home"** — a birth professional's actual words to Mongan. She asks: are they saying that if you want a normal birth, you don't belong in a hospital? That hospitals are only for people who don't care if they are not treated with kindness and care?
- **Resigned submission** — many parents end up doing what they said they did not want because some doctors will not even discuss their requests; they end up in "resigned submission" instead of seeking a different option

## 4. Emotional Consequences of Dismissive Care

- **Mourning a lost birth vision** — many mothers leave their births overwhelmed with grief, mourning the loss of the vision they held for their birthing because, in many cases, they have been denied their vision
- **Link to postnatal distress** — Mongan argues that if birth professionals would open their hearts and minds and implement protocols that make birthing less traumatic, there would be far less need for remediating postpartum depression with pills. The answer to avoiding birthing PTSD is "hiding in plain sight"
- **The contrast in HypnoBirthing mothers** — mothers who experience calm, natural births are so exuberant that they feel they could take on any obstacle. Dads report that the experience has touched them deeply and that they are closer as a couple than they have ever been
- **Consumer-led change** — the number of routine interventions and surgical births will continue to reach astronomical levels until parents, the consumers of birth services, speak out and let their wishes be known; birth professionals need to listen, truly see, and consciously evaluate the births they attend
