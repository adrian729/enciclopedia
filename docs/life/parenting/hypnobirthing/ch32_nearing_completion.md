## 1. What Happens at the End of the Thinning and Opening Phase

- **Surges become closer, stronger, and more beneficial** — the cervical wall has completely thinned and the cervix continues to open to allow the baby to begin moving down
- **Transition from Slow Breathing to Birth Breathing** — when the body signals downward fullness, it is time to switch from Slow Breathing (breathing up with the surge) to Birth Breathing (gentle but firm breathing down). This bypasses the lengthy, difficult, and fatiguing "pushing" techniques used by other methods
- **Natural expulsive reflex (NER) takes over** — the body's own pulsations move the baby down. It is critical to do nothing and let the body do its job. The vaginal wall lubricates and expands to assist

## 2. The Mother's Experience

- **Time distortion sets in** — you lose track of time; twenty minutes will feel like five. This is nature's way of keeping you placid and serene
- **Amnesiac, dreamlike state** — you drift in and out of alertness, conversations in the room become fuzzy, and responding feels like too much effort. This is a sign of deep instinctive birthing, not distress
- **Mood remains calm and nearly euphoric** — awareness narrows to the baby and the birthing body. The birthing companion's touch and voice guide you through each surge
- **No pathological reason for discomfort** — from this point on, discomfort arises only if you slip out of the relaxed state and allow tension to build in the body

## 3. How to Participate

- **Lateral position is common** — many women have already adopted it by this stage. If you stay on your back, the head of the bed must be elevated; lying flat can limit oxygen supply to the baby. The birthing companion should watch for you sliding down and help adjust
- **Let go and let your body work** — continue breathing up with surges until the cervix fully opens. With the signal of downward lower fullness, instinctively switch to Birth Breathing
- **Birthing companion's role** — assure staff that the mother is not pushing; she is safely switching to Birth Breathing
- **Do not leave the mother alone** — though she may appear perfectly relaxed and simply resting, she may be actively breathing her baby down to the vaginal outlet. That calm appearance can be deceiving

## 4. The Pelvic Station

- **Measuring the baby's descent** — the Pelvic Station indicates where the top of the baby's head ("presenting part") sits relative to the midsection of the pelvis

| Station | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Negative numbers (e.g., -1) | Baby's head is above the pelvic midsection ("head is high") |
| 0 | Head is engaged at the pelvic midsection |
| Positive numbers (e.g., +1, +2) | Baby's head is below the pelvic midsection, progressing toward birth |
