## 1. Warning Signs That Require Medical Attention

- **Going past your due date alone is not cause for intervention** — but genuine medical risks in late pregnancy must not be dismissed; call your care provider at the earliest possible time if you experience any of the following

| Signal | What to watch for |
|---|---|
| Premature labour | Three or more weeks early |
| Diminished foetal motion | For more than six hours |
| Membrane release without labour | Prolonged period after membranes release with no natural start of labour |
| Persistent severe headaches | Possibly elevated blood pressure |
| Meconium in amniotic fluid | Strong odour, colour, or significant amount of greenish-brown, tarlike substance |
| Excessive vaginal bleeding | — |
| Prolapsed cord | Get off your feet, raise your buttocks, call 999 immediately |
| Infection or fever | Any indication of either |
| Persistent vomiting or diarrhoea | Excessive or unrelenting |
| Dizziness or blurred vision | — |
| Severe swelling | Hands, face, ankles, or feet |
| Darkened urine | Considerably darkened |
| Unrelenting anxiety | Persistent worry over mother's or baby's well-being |
| Suicidal thoughts | Call 999 immediately |

## 2. Natural Methods to Initiate Labour

- **Hot and spicy foods** — Mexican, Indian, or Italian dishes with strong spice can stimulate the digestive system, which in turn stirs the birthing muscles into action; combine with other techniques for best effect
- **Lovemaking ("hugs before drugs")** — semen contains prostaglandin, which softens the cervix; kissing, hugging, fondling, and gentle nipple or clitoral stimulation trigger oxytocin production that can start uterine surges; bringing the mother to orgasm is helpful. Stimulate both nipples simultaneously if one is not enough, but avoid prolonged or vigorous stimulation of both as it can cause hyperstimulation harmful to the baby
- **Visualisation during stimulation** — while nipple or clitoris is being stimulated, use the rosebud visualisation (focusing on the bud slowly unfolding and opening) and gently direct breath down into the vaginal region
- **Walking** — extended walking can help initiate labour
- **Warm bath or shower** — a medium-hot bath provides relaxation; scoop water over nipples and abdomen, or direct hot shower water over the abdomen
- **Fear release session** — have the birthing companion guide a fear-release session to identify lingering fears or unresolved emotions; locked-up emotions produce catecholamine, which tenses the cervix and blocks natural relaxants. Call your HypnoBirthing instructor for a professional session if needed
- **Acupressure** — a professional acupressurist can facilitate natural onset of labour almost immediately; do not apply pressure to labour-inducing points at any time other than when you are ready to initiate labour
- **Acupuncture / auricular therapy** — an acupuncturist or auricular therapist can activate specific points for effective, smooth induction; as with acupressure, these points must not be stimulated during pregnancy except for induction purposes
- **Homeopathic and naturopathic support** — seek these services in collaboration with the methods above when other techniques have not been sufficient

## 3. If Artificial Induction Becomes Necessary

- **Last resort only** — artificial induction by Pitocin/Syntocinon drip should be considered only after all natural methods have been exhausted
- **Request minimal dosage** — ask that only a minimal dose be administered and that it not be increased without your consent
- **Ask for withdrawal once labour takes over** — request that the Pitocin/Syntocinon be stopped once your body has established its own labour rhythm
- **HypnoBirthing can still work** — many mothers report that their relaxation techniques carried them through even an induced labour, but artificial induction should be a last resort
