## 1. Choosing Your Techniques

- **No need to master all techniques** — once you can bring breathing into a smooth, rhythmic pace and ease into relaxation effortlessly, pick whichever methods below work best for you; they are interchangeable tools, not a required sequence

## 2. Progressive Relaxation

- **Body-part numbering** — assign each body region (head to toes) a number from the provided illustration; take a deep breath, then exhale and let relaxation flow down through each numbered zone in sequence, causing muscles to go totally limp
- **"Lucy Limp" target state** — the goal is a body as floppy as a soft doll (like a Beanie Baby); head hangs loose and forward, arms and hands fall limp at your sides
- **Speed conditioning** — with practice, you can count off all numbers in a single exhale; the more rapidly you think the numbers, the more rapidly you feel the effects, bringing the body immediately into a limp state

## 3. Disappearing Letters

- **Rapid alphabet technique** — close your eyes, take a quick deep breath, pause, then exhale while mentally reciting "AAA-BBB-CCC-D..." and visualising the letters rolling by. Allow your head, neck, shoulders, and upper torso to instantly sink into the frame of your body
- **Self-terminating** — by the first or second "C," most people find the remaining letters erase from their mind because it becomes too much effort to continue; the body goes limp on its own
- **Versatile use** — works as a quick calm-down during stressful moments at work or home, as a sleep aid (you will feel your head creating a deeper indentation into your pillow as neck muscles release), and is especially recommended during the postpartum adjustment period

## 4. Light Touch Massage

- **Endorphin mechanism** — the birth companion uses the back of the fingers to lightly stroke the mother's skin; this stimulates the erector pili (smooth muscle just below the skin surface), which contracts and raises surface hair (goose bumps), triggering endorphin release. The key point: endorphins and catecholamines cannot co-exist, so creating endorphins blocks the stress hormones that cause tension and pain
- **V-pattern and figure-eight motions** — (1) place the back of the fingers at the base of the tailbone and draw them up and out from the spine in a V shape, continuing upward to the base of the neck and around to the ears; (2) same starting point but work upward in a horizontal figure-eight pattern that criss-crosses at the centre of the back. The undersides of the arms and around the elbows are particularly effective areas
- **Nipple stimulation caution** — extending the massage around the sides of the breasts and nipples produces both endorphins and oxytocin (which enhances uterine surges). For this reason, do not include nipple stimulation until approaching the very end of the pregnancy term
- **Positioning during labour** — mother can sit on a birth ball leaning on pillows at the side of a bed, kneel on the bed resting arms and head on a pillow at the raised head of the bed (keeps her upright for baby's descent), or kneel on a pillow in front of a chair or sofa at home. The birth companion kneels behind her
- **Not for infants** — Light Touch should not be used on infants whose nervous system is not yet sufficiently developed; it can be introduced after three or four months to calm a fretful child

## 5. Anchoring

- **What an anchor is** — a lasting imprint planted in the subconscious through association with a gesture, sound, image, or touch; once established, the anchor triggers the associated response automatically
- **Shoulder-press anchor for birthing** — during practice sessions, the birth companion places a hand on the mother's shoulder with a gentle downward press while instructing her that she will immediately relax twice as deeply. Repeated practice makes this response automatic, so during labour the shoulder press alone can rapidly deepen relaxation
- **Broad applicability** — anchors can be set for any behaviour; the book gives the example of a woman who anchored a vitamin reminder to picking up her car keys and never missed a day afterward
