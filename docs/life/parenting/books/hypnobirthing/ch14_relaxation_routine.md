## 2. Why Daily Practice Is Non-Negotiable

- **Conditioning requires discipline** — just as a skier would never compete without physical conditioning, a birthing mother cannot attend classes and hope relaxation will occur on its own; daily rehearsal is what makes these techniques automatic when labour begins
- **Mind and body are inseparable** — stress and tension in the mind directly produce stress and tension in the body; athletes appear to perform effortlessly precisely because they have conquered this link through practice
- **Practice shortens labour** — the time invested in conditioning can cut both the duration and the effort of labour; the mind forms a pattern it can automatically respond to when birthing starts

## 3. Setting Up Your Practice Environment

- **Same time, same place** — dedicate a specific time each day and use the same comfortable, softly lit spot so the routine itself becomes a relaxation cue
- **Eliminate interruptions** — turn off the phone ringer and answering machine; this is protected time
- **Warmth and comfort** — use a soft throw over your body and wear non-binding clothes; warmth is essential for relieving tension
- **Empty bladder** — always empty your bladder before starting
- **Use HypnoBirthing audio** — CDs or MP3s with consistent background music (Stephen Halpern's *Comfort Zone* is recommended because it contains tones and rhythms the body responds to best and matches the Rainbow Relaxation)

## 4. Recommended Positions

- **Let your body choose** — use whichever position feels most comfortable; the two recommended positions are Nesting and the Lateral (side-lying) position
- **Avoid lying flat on your back in later pregnancy** — the weight of the baby can compress the inferior vena cava, restricting blood and oxygen flow to the baby; elevate the upper body instead

**Nesting setup:**
- One or two pillows under head and shoulders
- Shoulders drifting downward and outward
- One pillow under each arm, elbows slightly bent, fingers softly cupped
- Pillows under each knee bend
- Feet about six inches apart, turned outward, relaxed

**Lateral (side-lying) setup:**
- Lie on the left side; left shoulder, neck, and head resting on a pillow; left arm placed loosely at side
- Right arm resting on the pillow with elbow bent
- Left leg straight down with a slight knee bend
- Right leg bent up with knee at abdomen level, supported by one or two pillows

## 5. Facial Relaxation Technique

- **Jaw-vaginal connection** — the lower jaw area directly affects the vaginal opening; when the jaw is relaxed, the vaginal area relaxes too
- **The technique** — let eyelids close gently (do not force); sense the natural drooping of eye muscles and let relaxation spread from forehead, across eyelids, over cheekbones, and around jaws; let the lower jaw recede and teeth part; place the tip of the tongue where the upper teeth meet the palate to connect with the body's energy orbit; feel the head sinking into the pillow as neck, shoulders, and elbows droop; picture shoulders opening outward and sinking into the body frame
- **Goal: instant trigger** — once mastered, facial relaxation becomes an instant entry point into full-body relaxation, usable at any moment during labour
