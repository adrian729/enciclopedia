## 1. Core Premises

- **The body already knows how to birth** — in the absence of a special medical circumstance, a woman's body should not be forced to do what it already knows how to do; the design is perfect and needs to be respected and unimpaired
- **Individual treatment, not blanket protocol** — mothers and babies should be treated according to the status of their own health, not according to protocols designed for women who have special needs
- **Each intervention carries a cost** — every needlessly imposed procedure, test, exam, or interruption can upset the natural rhythm and flow of labour and may cause the birthing to go askew
- **Labour has its own timetable** — a labour may rest or accelerate; it should not be externally manipulated or managed unless a special circumstance requires it
- **Bodily autonomy persists during birth** — women's bodies are as sacrosanct during birthing as at any other time or in any other setting

## 2. What the Programme Teaches

- **Educational framework** — the programme is built on self-understanding, special breathing techniques, relaxation, visualisation, meditative practice, attention to nutrition, positive body toning, and healthful family living
- **Philosophy over technique** — HypnoBirthing is more a philosophy of how babies should be welcomed into the world than it is a "method"; its basic tenet is that birth is a celebration and should be experienced calmly, gently, joyfully, and comfortably
- **Mind-body connection** — when the mind is free of stress and fear, the body does not respond with pain, and nature is free to process birth in the same well-designed manner it handles all other normal physiological functions
- **Not a promise of zero pain** — HypnoBirthing does not profess to offer preparation for births totally free of pain, discomfort, or unanticipated incidents; it offers women the opportunity to explore the possibility of stepping into birthing without fear or stress

## 3. The Role of the Birthing Family

- **Birth is about the family, not the institution** — birth is a natural extension of the sexuality of a man and woman and is fundamentally about them; it is about family fulfilment and the life-changing transition into parenthood
- **Restoring the birth companion's role** — the programme helps birth companions free themselves from century-old programming that eroded their role and made them onlookers in one of the most important experiences of their lives
- **Learning about birth, not being taught how to birth** — families don't need to be taught how to birth; they need to learn about birth so they can approach it as informed consumers who see through medical and media myths
- **Routine intervention vs. necessary intervention** — the philosophy does not preclude the introduction of medical intervention; it precludes the introduction of routine, arbitrary, unnecessary intervention

## 4. Birth as a Whole-Life Event

- **Babies remember birth** — research by Dr. David Chamberlain (author of _Babies Remember Birth_) shows that children and adults can recall events from the womb and birth, sometimes vividly; how a baby is received into the world leaves an indelible imprint
- **The prenatal period matters** — parents are responsible for ensuring calm, happy, wholesome womb life; the baby's nutritional, emotional, and safety needs begin before birth
- **First birthday is the most important** — no single event lingers longer in a woman's mind than her birthing day; for the baby, the first moments of life can teach love, respect, and protection — or indifference
- **Post-birth adjustment** — HypnoBirthing extends beyond birth into the adjustment period, when parents help the baby establish a foundation of social and emotional responses that will last throughout life

## 5. When Special Circumstances Arise

- **Techniques complement any birth path** — if pregnancy or birth requires special considerations, HypnoBirthing techniques help the mother remain calm, confident, and stress-free regardless of the path her birthing takes
- **Applies to surgical births and VBAC** — many women with special circumstances, including those who required surgical births or planned Vaginal Birth After Caesarean, have enjoyed happier and easier births as a result of HypnoBirthing preparation
- **The 95-percent frame** — the programme is designed especially for the 95 percent of families whose pregnancies fall into normal, low-risk, or no-risk categories, but it benefits anyone who prepares with it
