## 1. Role of Visualisation in the Programme

- **Supplementary tools, not daily essentials** — breathing and relaxation are the fundamental daily practices; visualisation exercises are labour-day tools you experiment with to find what works for you. The single exception is Rainbow Relaxation, which should be practised every day
- **Conditioning through repetition** — during deep relaxation the brain and nervous system become saturated with the image of your goals; repeated visualisation imprints on the subconscious, creating acceptance, belief, and confidence in the desired outcome (calm, gentle birthing)

## 2. Rainbow Relaxation

- **Cornerstone exercise** — the primary daily visualisation combining colour imagery with deep relaxation. Background music ("The Comfort Zone" by Steven Halpern) is designed to bring thoughts into harmony with the body's natural energy flow
- **Technique** — use Calm Breathing to enter relaxation, then picture yourself resting on a bed of strawberry-coloured mist about a foot and a half high. Visualise the mist flowing through and around your body until you feel almost weightless and melting into it. Progress through each colour of the rainbow (strawberry, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo) and end with white for clarity. For each colour, let the mist sweep from the top of the head down to the tingling soles of the feet, deepening relaxation with each layer
- **"Falling asleep" is not a problem** — if you drift off or lose track of the material during practice, you are not actually asleep; you have conditioned your mind to respond so quickly that you enter a state that feels like sleep. Your subconscious continues to process the session. This is part of the conditioning effect
- **Substitute freely** — if any words or images on the recording feel uncomfortable, mentally replace them with something that suits you better and let the substitution deepen your relaxation. All hypnosis is self-hypnosis; no one brings you into the state except yourself
- **Birth companion's active role** — the companion should practise Rainbow Relaxation with the mother as often as possible, reciting the colour sequence while stroking her hand and arm in a soft downward motion. Keep joint sessions shorter but more frequent to prevent them from becoming a chore. This conditions the mother to deepen relaxation on hearing her companion's voice, and it familiarises the baby with that voice for post-natal bonding

## 3. The Birth Companion's Reading

- **Alternative to Rainbow Relaxation** — an adapted script (originally by Henry Leo Bolduc from *Self-Hypnosis: Creating Your Own Destiny*) that the birth companion reads aloud, combining guided imagery of ocean waves, surges, endorphin release, and the moment of birth
- **Key imagery within the reading** — the mother visualises ocean waves (ebb and flow mirroring surges and release), breathes in her body's natural endorphins, and pictures herself stepping into the joyful scene of holding and bonding with her baby moments after birth. This final visualisation creates an imprint of a positive, happy outcome
- **Perinatal bonding emphasis** — the reading frames birth as a spiritual event and addresses both the mother and the baby, reinforcing that the attitudes and philosophy communicated during pregnancy are gentle suggestions the child absorbs during birthing

## 4. The Opening Blossom

- **Rose visualisation for the perineum** — use breathing techniques to enter relaxation, then envision the baby moving gently down to the vaginal outlet and picture the gradual opening of the perineum as the gentle unfolding of the petals of a rose
- **When to use** — recommended during the final days of pregnancy to help achieve the onset of labour, and during both the opening and birthing phases of labour

## 5. Blue Satin Ribbons

- **Cervical thinning visualisation** — close your eyes and reimagine the lower circular muscle fibres of the cervix not as fibres but as soft, blue satin ribbons that gently and easily yield to the rhythmic draw of the vertical muscles, swirling up and back
- **When to use** — practise toward the end of pregnancy so the image is available during the surges of the thinning and opening phase of labour

## 6. Arm-Wrist Relaxation Test

- **Proof that practice is working** — because self-hypnosis does not produce an obvious "sensation," this test provides tangible, visible confirmation for both the mother and the birth companion that their conditioning is effective
- **Technique** — lie on your back with arms at your sides and fingers gently cupped. Enter relaxation, then picture the birth companion tying a giant, helium-filled red balloon to your right wrist. Feel the tug. Visualise more balloons being added (orange, yellow, and on through the rainbow colours); with each balloon your arm feels lighter and rises higher despite your efforts to hold it down. When the arm rises six to ten inches, place it back at your side
- **Progressive conditioning** — each time you practise, fewer balloons are needed to lift the arm. End each session by telling yourself that relaxation will take over sooner than ever before; the goal is to reach deep relaxation within a very short time
- **Caution** — do not lie flat on your back for long periods, especially in late pregnancy or in labour
