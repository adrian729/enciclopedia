## 1. Crowning and Final Surges

- **Visible results of labour** — this is the first time you see the tip of the baby's head. Natural body pulsations slowly urge the baby forward as you continue Birth Breathing
- **One or two more surges after full crowning** — once the top of the head is fully visible, usually only one or two additional surges are needed to gently birth the head. The perineum stretches with elastic-like flexibility if the mother stays relaxed
- **Preventing tears** — practise perineal massage beforehand and avoid rushed, violent pushes. The birthing companion continues prompts and helps the mother return to relaxation between surges, keeping the entire pelvic area as relaxed as possible
- **Direct breath toward the vagina** — breathing toward the opening and helping the baby move forward allows the perineum to unfold naturally

## 2. Immediate Post-Birth Handling

- **Birth the baby fully before routines** — if everything is fine, suctioning and other procedures should wait until the baby has been completely birthed
- **Parents receive the baby** — one of the parents should receive the baby if they wish. Handling by anyone other than the parents should be minimal and only if necessary, to make the baby's transition less traumatic
- **No rush to clean the baby** — the vernix caseosa (the cheesy covering on the newborn's skin) is a gift from nature; it absorbs naturally into the baby's skin, and any excess is removed later at the baby's first bath
- **No rush to cut the cord** — skin-to-skin contact with both parents takes priority over cord cutting. The birthing companion announces the baby's sex, and the family shares this moment together while caregivers visually assess the baby's condition

## 3. Skin-to-Skin Bonding

- **Immediate skin contact** — the baby is placed on the mother's bare chest or abdomen right after birth. Kangaroo Mother Care studies (from Australia) have found that the mother's body heat adjusts to the needs of her newborn
- **Birthing companion's role** — place a hand on the baby's back for skin-to-skin bonding; the baby needs to feel safe among the people whose scents and energies she is most familiar with
- **Bonding should not be rushed** — through caresses, gaze, and soft conversation, the parents validate the infant's acceptance. The baby feels this love, and her feelings of security and self-worth are validated
- **A lasting emotional imprint** — the natural high experienced during these first moments stays with both parents for the rest of their lives. This is where the prenatal relationship is reaffirmed through actual physical contact

## 4. First Breastfeeding

- **Babies are genetically programmed to take to the breast** — like all mammals, newborns instinctively crawl toward the breast. Allowing the baby to complete this crawl has both physical and psychological benefits
- **Uterine benefits of breastfeeding** — stimulation at the breast causes the uterus to contract, helps expel the placenta, and closes blood vessels to prevent excessive bleeding
- **Midwife support** — the midwife will offer suggestions and assistance with the first feeding while the birthing companion supports the baby's body or participates in cutting the cord
