## 1. Instinct as the Birthing Guide

- **"Doing nothing" means letting the body lead** — the programme does not teach you to use your mind to direct birth; it teaches you to quiet your mind and get out of the way so your body's natural instinct can take over
- **Instinct is involuntary and precise** — related words include spontaneous impulse, inner sense, reflex, compulsion, predisposition, and "a knowing that is not based on experience or learning"; just as you pull your hand from a hot object without thinking, your body knows how to birth without conscious instruction
- **Instinct has operated your whole life** — from your first breath at birth, to communicating hunger as an infant, to puberty, your body performed complex functions with no teaching; birthing is no different
- **Comatose mothers have birthed unassisted** — since 2009 in the United States, seven women in complete comas gave birth without assistance, demonstrating that human birthing instinct is fully intact even when consciousness is absent
- **Dr. Michel Odent's principle** — "You cannot improve upon a natural function. The answer is not to hinder it." Give your body permission to take the lead rather than trying to direct it

## 2. How Medical Culture Overrides Instinct

- **Confidence erosion during pregnancy** — well-intentioned friends, family, and care providers bombard the expectant mother with frightening birth stories and the message that her body is flawed, stripping her of the trust she has always had in herself
- **Society frames pregnancy as fragility** — the pregnant woman is cast as vulnerable and hopelessly emotional, requiring constant advice and intervention, rather than as a vital, healthy woman equipped by nature
- **Doctors are also taught away from instinct** — many medical graduates enter practice never having witnessed a normal, instinctive, unmedicated birth; some medical schools advocate surgical delivery as the preferred method
- **The standard second-stage protocol** — at full dilation, the calm birthing environment is disrupted: additional staff arrive, the bed is "broken down," the mother is placed in stirrups, and she is coached to forcefully push (the Valsalva manoeuvre) for up to two-plus hours, exhausting both mother and baby

## 3. Why HypnoBirthing Does Not Use Forced Pushing

- **Forced pushing is counterproductive** — it causes the vaginal sphincters to close ahead of the descending baby, creates stress, and recent studies suggest it can damage the pelvic floor
- **Oxygen deprivation** — when the mother is told to hold her breath and push, the baby is deprived of much-needed oxygen, causing a change in the baby's heart rate
- **Calm descent is possible** — many HypnoBirthing mothers experience the birthing phase as calmly as the opening phase, remaining in the position of their choice and allowing the baby's descent to begin almost unnoticed; because there is no visible change in behaviour, only a trained eye detects that birthing is happening
- **Birth preferences matter here** — the moves during this phase should follow the requests stated in your birth preferences so you are not swept into procedures that contradict what your instinct and preparation dictate
