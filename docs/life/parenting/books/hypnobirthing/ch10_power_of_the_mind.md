## 1. The Four Laws of the Mind

Four Laws of the Mind form the basis of HypnoBirthing's approach to changing beliefs about birth:

- **Law of Psycho-Physical Response** — for every thought, suggestion, or emotion, there is a corresponding physiological and chemical response in the body. The body is the "robot" that acts out whatever the mind perceives as real (Krazner's Robot Theory). If the mind dwells on fearful birth images, the body enters defence mode and tenses; if the mind holds calm, positive images, the body releases endorphins and relaxes. This is the most important Law of the Mind for birthing
- **Law of Repetition** — the more a single thought is expressed (mentally or aloud), the more deeply it becomes ingrained and the more readily the mind accepts it as reality. This is why daily practice of birthing affirmations and the Rainbow Relaxation recording is essential — occasional exposure does not create conditioning
- **Law of Harmonious Attraction** — what you put out in thought and speech creates energy that returns to you in the same form ("echo effect" or "boomerang effect"). Negative self-talk and frightening birth language attract negative experiences; positive affirmations and gentle language attract calm outcomes. Frequency of a message does not equal fact — the widespread association of pain with childbirth is conditioned, not inherent
- **Law of Motivation** — when the mind is highly motivated, the body responds accordingly. A football player with a sprained ankle feels no pain while focused on winning the game; a young woman in secret labour at a prom births quickly and quietly because her fear of detection overrides any expectation of a long, difficult labour. Motivation can also work against you: a "secondary benefit" (e.g., craving attention) can unconsciously create a difficult pregnancy or dramatic birth

## 2. The Power of Language

- **Words create a physiological chain reaction** — words trigger thoughts and emotions; repeated thoughts become feelings; feelings become beliefs; beliefs shape behaviour; behaviour shapes experience. Positive words create positive experiences, and vice versa
- **Negative imprints are lasting** — "sticks and stones" is physiologically false; caustic, belittling, or frightening words create lasting imprints. Tell a child learning to walk that he is clumsy, and he begins to feel and move clumsily; tell him his normal bodily functions are "smelly and disgusting," and he begins to feel bad about his body. The more potent the thought, the more potent the imprint
- **Guard your environment** — associate with people who reinforce positive thinking about birth; if others want to share horror stories, suggest waiting until after the baby arrives. Do not get pulled into those conversations

## 3. Motivation and Self-Image

- **You birth the way you live** — a woman's approach to labour mirrors her general approach to life; self-image and intent directly determine how she makes choices about her birthing
- **Secondary benefit trap** — without conscious awareness, some mothers create high-maintenance pregnancies or dramatic births because the attention and sympathy are rewarding. Family members can reinforce this pattern by treating pregnancy as a frail, precarious state
- **Assess your model** — two models determine how you approach pregnancy and birth:

| Dependent Model | Whole-Person Model |

| Submissive | Powerful |

## 4. Understanding Self-Hypnosis

- **Not stage magic** — all hypnosis is self-hypnosis; it is impossible to make someone act against their morals or principles while in hypnosis. Stage volunteers are simply willing participants having fun
- **Already a daily experience** — losing track of time while absorbed in work, daydreaming, or emotionally reacting to a film are all naturally occurring hypnotic states
- **What it feels like during labour** — you will hear conversations and can join in or not; you will be fully in control and can interrupt or resume relaxation at will. You will be aware of surges but experience them comfortably. As labour advances, you will likely go deeper within, working in harmony with your baby
- **Recognised medical tool** — hypnosis was recognised by the American Medical Association in 1957 as a beneficial therapy; it is used in medicine, dentistry, and therapy for fears, chemotherapy effects, surgery preparation, and habit change
- **Practice is non-negotiable** — there is no benefit from attending classes alone. Daily practice with recordings and two-to-three weekly practice sessions with your birthing companion using provided scripts are required to reach the levels of relaxation that will be available to you during labour
- **Benefits beyond birth** — couples report that months of relaxation practice improve how they handle everyday situations and interact with each other; babies born to practising mothers are frequently described as "mellow" and better-natured
