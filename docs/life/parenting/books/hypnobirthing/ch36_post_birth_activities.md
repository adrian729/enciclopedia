## 1. Delayed Cord Clamping

- **Wait until pulsation stops** — cutting the cord while it is still pulsating abruptly severs the baby's blood and oxygen supply, depriving him of nutrients that affect lifelong health
- **Easier first breaths** — continued oxygen flow from the placenta while the cord still pulses allows the baby to begin breathing air gradually, making the transition gentler and more comfortable
- **Companion may cut the cord** — once pulsation has ceased and the cord is clamped, the birthing companion can participate in cutting the cord if desired

## 2. Birthing the Placenta

- **Allow natural expulsion** — one or two surges after the baby is born, the uterus continues to contract; these surges loosen the placenta from the uterine wall and help the uterus begin shrinking back to normal size
- **Timeframe: 5 to 30 minutes** — the process varies; if the placenta is not born within a reasonable time, the caregiver may suggest medical assistance, but the cord should never be pulled to extract it
- **Examination** — the midwife or doctor examines the placenta and then the abdomen to check the status of the uterus

## 3. Immediate Bonding and First Feed

- **Early breastfeeding** — the baby is put to the breast; HypnoBirthing babies, alert and comfortable, usually take to the breast within minutes of birth
- **Unmedicated babies self-attach** — Dr. Lennart Righard's 1990 Danish study showed that newborns whose mothers were not medicated during labour could crawl to the breast and suckle on their own, while medicated babies could not manage this even with assistance
- **Euphoria and bonding** — the body's response to the moment floods both parents with a feeling of joy and giddiness; parents and baby become absorbed in getting acquainted, often oblivious to the medical activities around them
