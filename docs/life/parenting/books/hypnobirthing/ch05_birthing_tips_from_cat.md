## 1. Squatter's Birth

- **The setting** — a stray cat Mongan named "Squatter" settled into the last nesting box in a chicken coop: dark, quiet, undisturbed, piled with straw. She chose the environment instinctively
- **Labour without distress** — Squatter lay flat on her side with legs stretched forward, purring throughout. A visible ripple ran along the side of her body; when it reached her outlet, each kitten's head slipped out easily. She showed no signs of pain or distress — this first-time mum's instincts were in full gear
- **Self-sufficient process** — when Mongan reached out to pat Squatter during labour, the cat abruptly knocked her hand away and resumed deep relaxation. The touch had interrupted her focus; she needed no assistance and made that clear

## 2. Instinctive Birthing Behaviours

- **Dark, secluded space** — Squatter selected the very last nesting box at the far end of the coop, where there was very little light, piled high with straw. She chose the darkest, most private spot available
- **Deep relaxation throughout** — the cat purred continuously during labour, remaining still and undisturbed. She had an almost cavalier manner about what she was experiencing
- **No assistance needed or wanted** — no one taught Squatter how to birth; her body completed the process on its own. Squatter's birthing reinforced Mongan's early convictions and gave her insights that the evidence that birth can be beautiful and simple may be a needlessly well-kept secret, but it is there

## 3. Fear Shuts Down Labour

- **Fight-or-flight in action** — when dogs suddenly fought in the yard, Squatter was startled and afraid; she instantly shut down her labour, grabbed one kitten in her mouth, and fled through a hole in the coop wall. She returned fifteen minutes later for the second kitten and later birthed two more kittens in a safer location
- **The human parallel** — Squatter instinctively shut down her labour and brought her babies to safety when she was startled and afraid. Human birthing mothers are not able to escape when they are afraid, yet the fear is just as real
- **Caregivers hold the key** — the people who could effect a change in the level of fear that women experience are the caregivers who routinely witness birth as a joyful and calm experience but also choose to dismiss it when it is there, hiding in plain sight
