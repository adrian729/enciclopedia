## 1. Hydration

- **Water is the single most important daily habit** — it transports nutrients to tissues, aids digestion and elimination, lubricates joints and muscles, and helps the kidneys filter toxins more efficiently during pregnancy
- **Amniotic fluid replenishes at least three times a day** — consistent water intake directly supports this process and helps prevent toxemia in late pregnancy
- **General guideline: eight 200 ml glasses daily** — increase if you live at higher elevation, in dry/hot climates, exercise heavily, or eat a high-fibre diet
- **Urine colour is your self-test** — properly hydrated urine looks like light lemonade; darker yellow means you need more water and more protein (pregnancy vitamins can alter the colour, so factor that in)
- **Water quality matters** — choose clean spring water or filtered water when possible

## 2. Nutritional Principles

- **You are the sole source of nutrition for your baby** — healthy mothers have fewer low-birthweight infants, fewer premature births, fewer cases of pregnancy-induced hypertension (PIH), and fewer incidences of toxemia
- **Supplements complement, not replace, a whole-food diet** — prenatal vitamins are additions to a strong nutritional foundation, not substitutes
- **Buy defensively** — choose fresh, organic produce over processed foods; learn which fruits and vegetables carry pesticide or preservative risks
- **Basic formula** — drink water, eat vegetables, consume 75-100 grams of protein daily, choose natural fats over processed industrial oils, eat whole foods, and "wipe out whites" (no white bread, rice, sugar, flour, starches — unless it is protein)
- **Perfection is counterproductive** — stress harms pregnancy, so incorporate what you can without turning nutrition into an anxiety source

## 3. Foods to Eat

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Protein (75-100 g/day)** | Cottage cheese, milk, eggs, butter, lean red meat, poultry, safe fish, peanut butter, tofu, nuts, seeds |
| **Safe fish / omega-3** | Check mercury risk before buying; avoid raw fish |
| **Green foods** | Dark leafy greens, broccoli, asparagus, avocado, Brussels sprouts, Swiss chard, collard greens (limit spinach — it can reduce calcium absorption) |
| **Orange foods** | Sweet potatoes, yams, squash, cantaloupe, oranges, peaches, carrots, pumpkin |
| **Red foods** | Watermelon, strawberries (if unsprayed), tomatoes, red raspberry leaf tea, cherries |
| **Quality salt** | Celtic, Mediterranean, or Pink Himalayan sea salt with no minerals removed |

- **"Clean 15" vs. "Dirty Dozen"** — if organic produce is unavailable, the Clean 15 (e.g., onions, avocados, pineapples) have skins chemicals cannot penetrate easily; the Dirty Dozen (e.g., strawberries, apples, spinach, grapes) absorb chemicals even after washing and peeling, so buy these organic when possible
- **Eating vegetables during pregnancy shapes the child's palate** — evidence suggests children whose mothers ate more vegetables in pregnancy find them more palatable later and develop fewer food aversions

## 4. Foods to Avoid

| Item | Reason |
|---|---|
| **Alcohol, caffeine, nicotine** | — |
| **Processed meats** | Hot dogs, luncheon meats, bologna, liverwurst |
| **Raw fish** | Oysters, sushi, and others |
| **Unnecessary fats** | Fried foods, French fries, fast foods |
| **White/refined foods** | Refined sugar, white flour products, white rice, white potatoes |
| **Sweets** | Candies with empty calories and no nutritional value |
