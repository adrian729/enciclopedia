## 1. Purpose and Use

- **Worksheet provided by practitioners** — your HypnoBirthing practitioner supplies these sheets as a template for designing your birth preferences; not all items will apply to every facility or geographic area
- **Complete before touring** — fill out the sheets before touring your birthing facility so you can discuss specific items with the person conducting your tour
- **Distribute copies** — attach the completed sheets to your prenatal record and provide additional copies to hospital admissions, your midwife, and birthing clinic staff; request that the information be shared with any physician, nurse, or midwife who may attend if your primary provider is unavailable
- **Flexibility built in** — the sheets explicitly acknowledge that preferences may change as labour unfolds and that full cooperation is given if a genuine special circumstance arises, after explanation and discussion

## 2. Communicating with Providers

- **Framing as partnership** — the cover letter thanks providers for being chosen and expresses trust that they will do their utmost to help attain a joyous, memorable, and satisfying natural birth
- **Special circumstances clause** — parents commit to full cooperation if a medical need arises, provided they receive a clear explanation, are told what options exist, and have a moment to discuss the decision between themselves
- **Birth companion inclusion** — the sheets request that the birthing companion be included in all discussions as labour advances, as he or she is fully prepared to support decisions and techniques

## 3. Preference Categories

The sheets organise requests into phases. Key items in each:

| Phase | Key Preferences |
|---|---|
| **Onset of labour** | Allow labour to begin naturally unless induction is truly needed for safety; remain at home until labour is well established |
| **Admission** | Return home if not yet 4-6 cm dilated; intermittent (not continuous) foetal monitoring; discuss "comfort level" rather than "pain level" or pain scales; dim lights and soft music; bed rails lowered for bonding |
| **Thinning and opening** | Freedom to walk, move, and choose positions; undisturbed rhythm with few or no vaginal exams; labour in tub or shower; remove BP cuff and monitor belts between readings; eat and drink as desired; no artificial rupture of membranes or augmentation without clear medical need; patience if labour slows; natural means before intervention for repositioning |
| **Descent** | Mother chooses her position; breathe baby down with prompts from birthing companion only; bear down only when the body is in surge, using the natural expulsive reflex |
| **Birth** | Allow baby to emerge physiologically without assist unless needed; suction airway only if medically necessary; parent receives baby; allow placenta to release naturally; artificial oxytocin only if clear indication of haemorrhage risk |
| **For baby** | Dry gently with soft fabric; place directly on mother's abdomen; delay cord cutting until pulsation ceases; allow baby to crawl to breast and self-attach; delay eye prophylaxis until after bonding; oral Vitamin K in multiple doses or delay injection; baby remains with parents at all times |
