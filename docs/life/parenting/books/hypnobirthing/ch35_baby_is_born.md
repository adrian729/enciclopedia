## 1. Atmosphere During the Birthing Phase

- **Calm announcement of crowning** — the appearance of the baby's head should be communicated quietly; loud, animated cheering can frighten the baby, who hears every sound
- **Not an athletic event** — birthing is not an athletic event; voices should not be raised, both for the mother's relaxation and for the baby's sensory comfort

## 2. Birth Breathing Technique

- **No pain expected in this phase** — the cervix is already thinned and open; the gradual descent, done without forcing, avoids strain on tissues and sphincters of the birth path
- **Natural expulsive reflex (NER)** — for much of the birthing phase, the body and baby work together through NER to move the baby down; the mother assists with Birth Breathing rather than directed pushing
- **Practise on the toilet** — the best rehearsal is during a bowel movement: notice the pulsations that move the stool down and out, then replicate that gentle, short-intake-and-nudge breathing pattern. This shows the technique works and builds muscle memory
- **Tongue placement** — rest the tip of the tongue where the front teeth meet the palate; this drops the lower jaw, releases tension in the mouth and jaw, and reflexively relaxes the vaginal outlet
- **J-shaped breath path** — take a short but deep breath through the nose, direct the energy to the lower back of the throat, then down through the body behind the baby in the shape of a "J" (down and forward), while letting the vaginal muscles open as though releasing the breath through them
- **Multiple breaths per surge** — repeat the J-breath several times with each surge as long as the body is surging; breathing down only once per surge wastes the surge's momentum, prolongs birthing, and drains energy
- **Keep breath energy directed downward** — do not let the thrust escape through the mouth; these are deep but not strenuous breaths with the energy going to the vagina
- **Birthing companion's role** — with each exhalation, the companion prompts the mother to breathe love and energy down to the baby, open the path, and nudge the baby toward birth

## 3. The Emergence

- **Head births first, then body follows** — the vulva gradually distends without discomfort; once the head is born, the body often requires only gentle bearing down
- **Deeply relaxed mothers may need none of the above** — some who have thoroughly mastered relaxation and deepening techniques find their body propels the baby through the birth path entirely on its own, without conscious Birth Breathing
