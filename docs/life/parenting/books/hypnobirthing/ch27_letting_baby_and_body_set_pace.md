## 1. Trusting the Natural Timeline

- **Labour's trigger is baby-initiated** — a hormone secreted from within the baby's body triggers oxytocin in the mother; the process has a designated flow but not a designated schedule
- **Slow or resting labour is not abnormal** — a latent period where surges do not start, or intervals between surges lengthen, simply means the uterus and baby are not yet ready; it does not indicate a complicated labour
- **Brain development continues late** — the baby's brain cell development accelerates during the last eight weeks of pregnancy, so rushing birth may cut short important neurological maturation
- **Artificial prompts can backfire** — sometimes even after accepting membrane rupture, vaginal gel, or a Syntocinon drip, labour still does not progress because the body is being pushed into a labour for which it is not ready

## 2. The Intervention Cascade

- **"Slippery slope" of augmentation** — once artificial induction or augmentation is introduced, you may surrender your choices; each intervention tends to require the next (Syntocinon leads to stronger surges, which leads to epidural request, which numbs sensation and impairs the mother's ability to work with surges, which prolongs labour, which leads to more Syntocinon)
- **Epidural side effects** — narcotics can reduce muscle tone, prolong labour, and mask surges so the mother cannot assist the birthing process efficiently; in extreme cases this cycle ends in surgical birth
- **Fever risk from epidurals** — epidurals can cause the mother to develop a fever, and once that happens there is little remedy except to deliver the baby quickly
- **Impact on the newborn** — foetology experts report that drug-induced disorientation in the baby can disrupt the mother-baby connection and create a long-term feeling of abandonment
- **PDR warning** — the Physicians' Desk Reference states there are no adequate, well-controlled studies on the use of labour drugs in pregnant women, and it remains unknown whether these drugs cause foetal harm

## 3. Protecting Your Birth Preferences

- **Respond to "speed up" talk calmly** — if augmentation is suggested without medical urgency, you or your birthing companion can explain that you prefer to stay with your birth preferences and are in no hurry
- **Vet your caregiver before labour** — the birthing room is not the place to discover your provider's philosophy clashes with yours; these conversations must happen during pre-natal visits
- **Hugs before drugs** — if induction talk arises, first try the natural methods covered in the next chapter (especially professional acupuncture, chiropractic, and acupressure); if already in labour but augmentation is being offered, ask for time and privacy to try natural approaches
- **Meet all diagnoses and recommendations with curiosity** — pause and consider the effects on both mother and baby; if the direction of your labour differs from what you envisioned, reassess the path you are on and consider making changes while you still can
