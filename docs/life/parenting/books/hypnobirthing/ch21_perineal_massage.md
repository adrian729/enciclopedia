## 1. Purpose and Timing

- **Improves the health, blood flow, elasticity, and relaxation of the pelvic floor** — one of the oldest and most reliable ways to prepare the perineal tissues for birth
- **Begin approximately six to eight weeks before your estimated due date** — daily sessions of at least five minutes
- **Teaches the muscles to relax and open outward under pressure** — during birth, a soft, well-massaged perineal rim gently unfolds and allows the baby to slip past with no resistance, significantly increasing the chance of an intact perineum
- **Familiarises you with the tissues involved** — you learn exactly which region will open during birth, reducing surprise and tension in the moment

## 2. Technique

- **Oil selection** — use virgin olive oil, sweet oil, almond oil, apricot oil, or a lubricating gel; avoid perfumed oils. Pour oil into a small cup and discard any leftover after each session (do not reuse)
- **Fingernail prep** — trim nails short and smooth; a rubber glove prevents rough surfaces from irritating vaginal tissue
- **Self-massage position** — place one foot on a chair seat with the other foot approximately two feet away; use your thumb to work around and under your abdomen from behind
- **Partner-assisted massage** — because your size makes bending awkward, a partner can perform the massage using the first two fingers; Mongan suggests making it part of lovemaking
- **Sitting position and mirror** — sit with your back against pillows and get comfortable. A mirror is helpful for the first few sessions to identify the muscles and observe the perineal edge softening
- **The motion** — insert the thumb or fingers (oiled to the second knuckle) approximately two to three inches into the vagina, press downward on the area between vagina and rectum, rub oil into the inner edge of the perineum and lower vaginal wall, then slide upward along the sides in a U-shaped sling motion; reach both the inner portions and the outer rim
- **Visualisation** — picture the perineum opening outward as pressure is applied; the "opening rosebud" image works well during this exercise
- **Expect initial tightness** — the muscles will feel tight at first but will progressively relax with consistent daily practice
