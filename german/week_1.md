## 1. German Pronunciation Guide

<a id="11-vowels"></a>

### 1.1. Vowels
- **a** - like "ah" in "father" [aː] (long) or "a" in "hat" [a] (short)
- **e** - like "eh" in "bed" [e] (short) or "ay" in "say" [eː] (long)
- **i** - like "ee" in "see" [iː] (long) or "i" in "bit" [ɪ] (short)
- **o** - like "oh" in "go" [oː] (long) or "o" in "hot" [ɔ] (short)
- **u** - like "oo" in "food" [uː] (long) or "u" in "put" [ʊ] (short)
- **ä** - like "eh" in "bed" [ɛ] (short) or "ai" in "hair" [ɛː] (long)
- **ö** - like "i" in "bird" [œ] (short) or "eur" in French "peur" [øː] (long)
- **ü** - like French "u" in "tu" [y] (short) or "ü" in German "über" [yː] (long)

<a id="12-consonants"></a>

### 1.2. Consonants
- **b, d, g** - at end of word: devoiced to [p, t, k] respectively (final devoicing)
- **ch** - after a, o, u, au: like Scottish "loch" [x] (e.g., "Bach")
- **ch** - after e, i, ä, ö, ü, ei, eu, äu: like "h" in "huge" [ç] (e.g., "ich")
- **sch** - like "sh" in "ship" [ʃ] (e.g., "schule")
- **sp, st** - at beginning of word: "shp", "sht" [ʃp, ʃt] (e.g., "spielen", "stehen")
- **sp, st** - elsewhere: normal [sp, st] (e.g., "aussprechen")
- **v** - like "f" [f] (e.g., "Vater")
- **w** - like "v" [v] (e.g., "was")
- **j** - like "y" in "yes" [j] (e.g., "Ja")
- **r** - guttural, uvular trill [ʁ] or vocalic [ɐ] at end of syllable
- **z** - like "ts" in "cats" [ts] (e.g., "Zeit")
- **s** - like "z" [z] between vowels, like "s" [s] elsewhere (except at end where it's voiceless)

<a id="13-special-combinations"></a>

### 1.3. Special Combinations
- **au** - like "ow" in "cow" [aʊ]
- **ei, ai, ay** - like "i" in "mine" [aɪ]
- **eu, äu** - like "oy" in "boy" [ɔʏ]
- **ie** - long "ee" [iː] (as in "Liebe")
- **eh** - long "e" [eː] (as in "gehen")

## 3. Basic Sentence Structure

<a id="31-word-order"></a>

### 3.1. Word Order
German main clauses follow: **Subject (Nominative) + Verb + Object (if any)**

<a id="32-definite-articles"></a>

### 3.2. Definite Articles
- **der** - masculine nominative
- **die** - feminine nominative
- **das** - neuter nominative
- **die** - plural nominative (all genders)

<a id="33-examples"></a>

### 3.3. Examples
- Der Mann geht. (The man goes.)
- Die Frau kommt. (The woman comes.)
- Das Kind spielt. (The child plays.)
- Die Hunde laufen. (The dogs run.)

<a id="5-pronunciation-drill"></a>

## 5. Pronunciation Drill

Read these sentences aloud, focusing on:
- Clear vowel length (long vs. short vowels)
- Final devoicing (b→p, d→t, g→k at end of words)
- Correct "ch" sounds
- Proper "r" pronunciation

1. Der Hund läuft. (deːɾ hʊnt lˈaʊ̯ft)
2. Die Frau isst. (diː fʁaʊ̯ ɪs̪t)
3. Das Kind schläft. (das kɪnt ʃlɛft)
4. Der Mann sieht das Haus. (deːɾ man ziːt das haʊ̯s)
5. Der Freund hilft der Frau. (deːɾ fʁœɪ̯nt hɪlft deːɾ fʁaʊ̯)
6. Das Buch ist gut. (das buːx ɪst ɡuːt)
7. Das Wasser ist kalt. (das vasɐ ɪst kalt)
8. Der Mann und die Frau gehen. (deːɾ man ʊnt diː fʁaʊ̯ ˈɡeːən)
9. Das Kind sieht den Hund. (das kɪnt ziːt deːn hʊnt)
10. Der Freund arbeitet heute. (deːɾ fʁœɪ̯nt ˈaʁbaɪ̯t ˈhɔʏ̯tə)

Practice each sentence slowly, then gradually increase speed while maintaining clear pronunciation.
